Prophet Samuel Ajayi Adegboyega

From humble beginnings in Gboyega Village to a global mantle of leadership and philanthropy.

Prophet Samuel Ajayi Adegboyega

Samuel Ajayi Adegboyega (born 22 June 1945) is a Nigerian pastor, prophet, philanthropist, and Christian leader who serves as the Founder, President, and First General Overseer of the Salvation Proclaimers Anointed Church (SPAC) Worldwide, headquartered in Ibadan, Oyo State, Nigeria. He is widely known for his commitment to prayer, holiness, prophetic ministry, and the transformative power of the Holy Spirit. Beyond his ministerial work, he is the founder of Focus for the Future (FFF), a philanthropic initiative dedicated to youth empowerment, educational advancement, and community development. Through FFF, hundreds of students receive scholarships annually, while many young people benefit from mentorship, leadership development, and financial support.

Early Life

Samuel Ajayi Adegboyega was born on 22 June 1945 in Gboyega Village, Orile Oko, Ogun State, Nigeria. His father, Adegboyega Lukotun Olowe, was a farmer, community leader, and founder of Gboyega Village, while his mother, Egbetoto Ilori Adegboyega, was a trader who later embraced Christianity after previously practising traditional Yoruba religion.

Born into a large family and raised in difficult economic conditions, Adegboyega's childhood was marked by poverty and hardship. Despite these challenges, he developed a strong desire for education and was supported by his mother's determination and sacrifices. To support his schooling, he engaged in various forms of petty trading and manual labour from an early age.

Education

Adegboyega displayed exceptional academic ability throughout his school years. He received double promotions during primary school because of his outstanding performance and eventually graduated from Ajebo Central School, Ogun State, in 1958.

In 1961, he gained admission to Lisabi Grammar School, Abeokuta. Despite severe financial challenges, he consistently excelled academically and remained one of the best students in his class. Through an uncommon academic advancement, he was promoted from Form Three directly to Form Five after passing a special examination. He completed his secondary education in 1964 with Grade One results.

Unable to attend university due to financial constraints, he pursued professional studies through correspondence education with Rapid Results College, London, successfully completing the examinations of the Institute of Chartered Secretaries and Administrators (ICSA).

"His professional career as a Chartered Secretary was marked by rare dedication, rising to become a Partner at Z.O. Ososanya & Co—a testament to the integrity that would later define his ministry."

Professional Career

Adegboyega began his career in 1965 as an accounts clerk at Lafia Hotel, Ibadan, and later worked at Premier Hotel. In 1970, he joined Sketch Publishing Company Limited, Ibadan, where he rose to become Administrative Manager and Secretary to the Board of Directors.

Following his departure from the organisation in 1975, he joined Z.O. Ososanya & Co. (Chartered Accountants) as an Administrative Officer. Through dedication and exceptional performance, he rose to become a Partner in the firm, a remarkable achievement for a Chartered Secretary in a chartered accounting practice. He remained with the firm until his retirement in April 2002, when he entered full-time ministry.

Conversion and Spiritual Journey

Although raised in a family influenced by traditional religion and Islamic practices, Adegboyega embraced Christianity in his youth and was baptised in 1960, adopting the name Samuel.

In July 1967, while attending Saint Paul's Apostolic Church in Ibadan, he experienced a life-changing spiritual encounter that strengthened his commitment to Christian service. Shortly afterwards, he publicly renounced practices he believed were inconsistent with his faith and entered into a personal covenant of holiness, discipline, and total devotion to God.

Ministry

Early Ministry

Adegboyega joined Saint Paul's Apostolic Church in 1967 and quickly became active in church leadership, serving as choirmaster and later as a pastor. His ministry became widely known for prayer, revival meetings, biblical teaching, and reports of spiritual transformation.

Following a major revival in 1977, differences emerged between his congregation and the church headquarters regarding the direction of the ministry. On 5 February 1978, his congregation separated and began operating independently.

Founding of SPAC

The ministry later became known as the Salvation Proclaimers Anointed Church (SPAC) Worldwide. Under Adegboyega's leadership, SPAC developed into a growing Pentecostal ministry committed to evangelism, holiness, discipleship, prayer, and community transformation.

A permanent cathedral was established along Polytechnic Road, Sango, Ibadan, becoming the international headquarters of the church. To support the work, Adegboyega personally sacrificed significant personal assets, including the sale of a residential property.

On 26 January 2002, he and his wife were formally consecrated for ministry by Pastor Enoch Adejare Adeboye, General Overseer of the Redeemed Christian Church of God.

About SPAC Worldwide

The Salvation Proclaimers Anointed Church (SPAC) Worldwide is a Pentecostal Christian ministry headquartered in Ibadan, Oyo State, Nigeria. The church is dedicated to preaching salvation through Jesus Christ, promoting holiness, prayer, evangelism, prophetic ministry, and the power of the Holy Spirit.

Over the years, SPAC has expanded across Nigeria with branches in Ibadan, Lagos, Abeokuta, and other locations, while also establishing an international presence through SPAC Nxtion Family in the United Kingdom. The ministry is actively involved in youth development, educational support, scholarships, welfare programmes, and community outreach.

Philanthropy

Beyond church ministry, Adegboyega is widely recognised for his commitment to humanitarian and educational causes. Through Focus for the Future (FFF), he has supported the education of hundreds of students through scholarship programmes and has invested in initiatives designed to empower young people through mentorship, leadership development, and financial assistance. His ministry also supports widows, vulnerable families, aspiring entrepreneurs, and students through various welfare and empowerment programmes.

Personal Life

Samuel Ajayi Adegboyega married Comfort Adedoyin Babalola in April 1970. Their marriage has been blessed with five children, including Pastor Tobi Adegboyega, who leads SPAC Nxtion Family in the United Kingdom.

He is known for his disciplined lifestyle, commitment to prayer and fasting, and emphasis on integrity, moral purity, and spiritual devotion. Throughout his ministry, he has consistently encouraged believers to live lives characterised by faith, holiness, and service to others.

Legacy

Prophet Samuel Ajayi Adegboyega is regarded as one of the notable Pentecostal church leaders to emerge from South-West Nigeria. His life story—from rural poverty to becoming a respected pastor, prophet, philanthropist, and church founder—has inspired many people across generations. Through SPAC Worldwide, Focus for the Future (FFF), and decades of ministry, he has influenced thousands of lives through spiritual leadership, education, youth empowerment, mentorship, and humanitarian service. His legacy continues through the churches, ministries, leaders, and individuals whose lives have been shaped by his teachings and example.
