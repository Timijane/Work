Timing Is Critical

Today's Word: Jun 16, 2026

Life is too short for you to live striving to force doors of opportunity to open, trying to convince someone to like you and working hard to win their approval, straining to manipulate a situation so you can win the upper hand. That’s going to wear you out and waste your time. David recognized that his times were in God’s hands. He was saying, “I’m not going to be frustrated by delays, or sour when it’s not working out the way I want, or discouraged because I’m not as far along as I thought I’d be. My God is in control.”

Timing is critical. You have to realize that you can do the right thing, but if it’s not the right time, it won’t work out. When it’s not the right time, no matter how hard you try, the door is not going to open. When you don’t feel a peace about it, when there is a sense of unrest in your spirit, you can’t make the wrong time into the right time. You have to come back to a place of peace and rest in God’s hand.

Joel Osteen • Tuesday, June 16, 2026 6:23 AM CT

Every morning declare, “Father, thank You that You created me in Your own image, that You crowned me with Your favor, that I’m a masterpiece, fearfully and wonderfully made. I am valuable, talented, worthy, a prized possession.” That’s exercising your spiritual authority. That’s marking your territory, setting new boundaries, letting the enemy know that the land belongs to you. When you pronounce it, then you can possess it. That’s when God will fight for you. You don’t have to defeat the enemy. He’s already defeated. God will go before you and clear the path. He’ll move the wrong things out of your way so you can possess the land.
